Annual Leave & Time Off Policy
Policy Number: POL-HR-004
Policy Owner: Human Resources Department
Effective Date: January 1, 2025


1. Executive Summary
Finance House is committed to promoting employee well-being and supporting work-life balance through a fair, transparent, and flexible leave policy. This Annual Leave & Time Off Policy defines entitlements, types of leave, processes, and compliance requirements for all permanent employees, ensuring consistency across bands and alignment with organizational and statutory norms. The policy covers annual leave, public holidays, sick leave, emergency leave, personal development leave, work-life balance initiatives, and compliance framework. The overarching goal is to facilitate rest, development, and personal needs while delivering operational continuity.


2. Leave Entitlements by Band
Leave entitlements are assigned based on employee classification, designed to provide adequate rest and opportunities for personal time.

2.1 Bands and Entitlements

Band	Annual Leave	Public Holidays	Example (2025)
Entry Level	20 days	8 days	20 + 8 = 28 days total
Mid-Level	22 days	8 days	22 + 8 = 30 days total
Senior Level	25 days	8 days	25 + 8 = 33 days total
Manager/Director	30 days	8 days	30 + 8 = 38 days total
2.2 Casual Friday Policy (Optional)

Finance House may offer "Casual Fridays" for eligible employees, permitting business-casual attire every Friday. Participation is voluntary and subject to manager discretion. Certain client-facing, compliance, or event-based roles may be excluded.


3. Types of Leave
3.1 Annual/Vacation Leave

Annual leave is intended for planned vacations, personal engagements, rest, and recreation. For example, an Entry Level employee receives 20 days annually, which may be taken in blocks or split throughout the year.

3.2 Sick Leave

10 days per calendar year, including for recovery from illness or injury.

Use permitted for physical or mental health.

3.3 Personal Emergency Leave

Employees may avail up to 2 days for unplanned emergencies (e.g., accidents, family crises).

3.4 Bereavement Leave

Up to 7 days permitted for the loss of immediate family member (parent, spouse, child).

3.5 Maternity/Paternity Leave

Maternity: 180 days paid

Paternity: 180 days paid

3.6 Adoption Leave

120 days paid (primary caregiver only)

3.7 Sabbatical Leave

Eligible after 5 years of service; up to 6 months, unpaid, subject to management approval for study, research, or volunteering.


4. Leave Request Process
4.1 Advance Notice

Submit annual or planned leave at least 2 weeks in advance via HRIS portal.

Example: For vacation beginning July 10, submit by June 26.

4.2 Manager Approval

All leave except sick and emergency requires manager approval.

Approval communicated within 3 working days.

4.3 Blackout Periods

No annual leave during month-end or year-end processing for finance roles (1st–5th of each month).

Exception: medical emergencies.

4.4 Calendar System Requirement

All leave requests and approvals to be tracked through official HRIS/calendar systems for transparency and audit.


5. Sick Leave
5.1 Immediate Notification

No advance notice required.

Notify manager by start of workday electronically.

5.2 Certification

Employees must provide a medical certificate for absences of 3 consecutive days or more.

5.3 Doctor's Appointment

Employees can avail 1–2 hours during workday for medical appointments.

Notify manager at least 24 hours prior if possible.

5.4 Mental Health Days

Employees permitted 3 mental health leave days per year, recorded as sick leave.

Example: Senior Level employee uses 2 mental health days, 8 standard sick days remain.


6. Emergency Leave
6.1 Definition

Personal emergencies demanding immediate attention, such as family crisis, accidents, or urgent caregiving.

6.2 Approval

Same-day notification and approval by manager.

Up to 2 days without documentation.

6.3 Documentation

Extensions beyond 2 days require evidence (doctor note, police report).


7. Carry-Over Policy
7.1 Maximum Carry-Over

Up to 5 annual leave days may be carried to next year.

7.2 Expiry

Unused carry-over days expire on December 31 of following year.

7.3 Payout

No cash payout for unused leave except on resignation.

7.4 Example Calculation

Employee with 3 unused annual days in 2025 may carry all 3 days to 2026.

Employee with 7 unused days may carry 5 only; 2 expire.

Leave balance on resignation: all accrued days paid out as per company payroll formula.


8. Leave During Notice Period
8.1 Resignation Notice

Employees in notice period may apply for leave; approval at manager’s discretion.

8.2 Final Payout

All accrued leave not availed during employment will be paid out at final settlement.


9. Public Holidays
9.1 Recognized Holidays

New Year's Day (January 1)

Republic Day (January 26)

Good Friday

Independence Day (August 15)

Gandhi Jayanti (October 2)

Diwali (date as per calendar)

Christmas (December 25)

Eid-Ul-Fitr (date as per calendar)

9.2 Holiday Fallback

If public holiday falls on employee’s scheduled leave, substitute leave may be taken post-holiday.

9.3 Example

Employee on annual leave during Diwali: eligible for one extra day post-vacation.


10. Leave for Personal Development
10.1 Training Leave

Up to 10 days per year for programs aligned with job role (e.g., certifications, workshops).

10.2 Manager Approval

Written request and program details required for approval.


11. Work-Life Balance Initiatives
11.1 Flexible Working Hours

Employees may propose adjusted working hours with manager approval.

11.2 Part-Time Options

Temporary part-time arrangements permitted with departmental approval for up to 6 months.

11.3 Work From Home During Leave

Employees cannot perform work tasks while on approved leave, except for pre-approved critical handover or emergencies.


12. Compliance and Violations
12.1 Leave During Notice

No special approval required for availing accrued annual leave.

12.2 Non-Utilization

Employees failing to use annual leave may be required to discuss work-life balance with their manager.

12.3 Leave Fraud

Any falsification of leave claims results in formal investigation and disciplinary action, up to and including termination.


13. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
Q1: How is annual leave accrued?
Annual leave is credited at the beginning of each calendar year per employee band.

Q2: Can unused sick leave be carried over?
No, sick leave cannot be carried over to the next year.

Q3: Can an employee use annual leave during a blackout period?
No, except for medical emergencies and with executive approval.

Q4: What if a public holiday falls during my scheduled leave?
You are entitled to a substitute day after your scheduled leave.

Q5: Are leave days paid out on resignation?
Yes, unused accrued leave is paid out at final settlement.

Q6: How do I apply for emergency leave?
Notify your manager and HR immediately. For up to 2 days, no documents required; extensions need evidence.

Q7: How is carry-over calculated?
Maximum of 5 days; any additional unused days expire on December 31. E.g., if you have 8 days left, 5 can be carried forward, 3 will expire.

Q8: What documentation is required for sick leave?
A medical certificate is needed if absence exceeds 2 consecutive days.

Q9: Is mental health covered under sick leave?
Yes, you are allowed up to 3 mental health days each year, counted as sick leave.

Q10: What is the process for sabbatical leave?
You must have completed 5 years. Apply in writing; management approval required. Unpaid and maximum of 6 months.

Q11: Can training leave be used for personal interests outside work?
No, training leave must relate to your job responsibilities or official skill development.

Q12: Can part-time work be continued indefinitely?
No, part-time arrangements are temporary (max 6 months) and subject to manager and HR review.


14. Examples and Calculations
Carry-Over Example:
An employee with 4 unused annual leave days at year-end 2025 will have all 4 days added to their 2026 leave balance. If the same employee has 8 unused days, only 5 will carry; the remaining 3 will expire on December 31, 2026.

Public Holiday Example:
If Independence Day falls during your approved annual leave, you may take one extra day after returning from vacation.

Notice Period Example:
An employee in notice period with 12 leave days remaining may apply for these during notice. If not used, all 12 will be paid out on exit.

Sabbatical Example:
An employee after 5 years seeks 3 months’ sabbatical. Upon manager approval and HR clearance, leave is taken unpaid; employee resumes work after agreed period.
